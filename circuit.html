First Circuit Diagram

  • The two 18650 batteries provide power to the PWM controller module.
  • The controller then regulates the amount of electrical power supplied to the DC motor by adjusting the PWM signal through the rotary knob.
  • When the knob is rotated, the motor speed increases or decreases, allowing the system to simulate different RPM conditions for the gas turbine engine mock-up.

Second Circuit Diagram

  • The circuit is controlled by an Arduino Uno, which acts as the main controller for the entire gas turbine engine mock-up system.
  • Power from the 18650 battery pack is regulated by the voltage converter before being supplied to the Arduino, motor driver, LCD display, LEDs, and other electronic components.
  • The potentiometer functions as the throttle control, where adjusting the knob changes the motor speed through the motor driver module.
  • The red LED indicates that the circuit is powered on and operating.
  • The blue LED lights up when the motor starts rotating.
  • The yellow LED turns on when the motor reaches its maximum speed.
  • The LCD display shows the throttle value and engine speed in real time for monitoring purposes.
  • The DFPlayer Mini module and speaker generate simulated aircraft engine sound effects to create a more interactive and realistic demonstration system.
